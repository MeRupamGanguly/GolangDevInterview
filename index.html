End-to-End Flow of a Payment Transaction

1. Customer Chooses Payment Method

Customer selects credit card, debit card, UPI, or wallet as a payment method on the merchant’s website or app (e.g., Amazon, Netflix).

→

2. Enter Payment Details

Customer enters payment information like card number, expiry date, CVV, or wallet details on the payment form.

Rupam Ganguly
→

3. Encryption with SSL/TLS

The payment details are encrypted using SSL/TLS to securely transmit the data over the internet.

Rupam Ganguly
→

4. Send to Payment Gateway

The encrypted payment details are sent from the merchant's website to the Payment Gateway for processing.

Rupam Ganguly
→

5. Tokenization

The Payment Gateway tokenizes or encrypts sensitive card data to avoid direct exposure of the customer’s payment information.

Rupam Ganguly
→

6. Forward to Payment Processor

The tokenized data is forwarded to the Payment Processor for authorization and validation of the transaction.

Rupam Ganguly
→

7. Payment Processor Validates

The Payment Processor checks if the merchant's account can accept payments and sends the transaction to the Card Network for further processing.

Rupam Ganguly
→

8. Forward to Card Network

The Payment Processor forwards the payment request along with the customer’s details to the Card Network (Visa, Mastercard, etc.) for authorization.

Rupam Ganguly
→

9. Card Network Routes to Issuer Bank

The Card Network routes the payment request to the Issuer Bank (the customer's bank) for validation.

Rupam Ganguly
→

10. Issuer Bank Validates

The Issuer Bank checks if the card is valid, the customer has sufficient funds, and runs fraud detection checks (e.g., 3D Secure, location, card status).

Rupam Ganguly
→

11. Authorization Response

The Issuer Bank sends an authorization response (approved or declined) back through the Card Network to the Payment Processor.

Rupam Ganguly
→

12. Forward Response to Payment Gateway

The Payment Processor forwards the authorization result (approved or declined) to the Payment Gateway.

Rupam Ganguly
→

13. Notify Merchant and Customer

The Payment Gateway notifies the merchant’s website and the customer of the transaction result. If approved, the customer sees a confirmation, and if declined, an error message is shown (e.g., insufficient funds, expired card).

Rupam Ganguly
→

14. Capture Payment

The merchant can initiate a capture request to collect the funds from the customer’s account. This can happen immediately or later based on the merchant's settings (e.g., delayed capture).

Rupam Ganguly
→

15. Funds Transfer

The Payment Processor sends a request to transfer the funds from the Issuer Bank to the Acquirer Bank (merchant's bank), minus transaction fees.

Rupam Ganguly
→

16. Transaction Complete

The payment is successfully completed, and the merchant receives the funds, completing the transaction process.
